Faculty Directory

Meet our distinguished faculty members who are dedicated to excellence in teaching and research

Our Faculty

Nexatech University boasts a diverse and accomplished faculty comprised of leading experts in their respective fields. Our faculty members are committed to providing students with a transformative educational experience through rigorous teaching, cutting-edge research, and mentorship.

With a blend of academic excellence and real-world experience, our faculty prepares students to become leaders and innovators in their chosen professions.

Faculty Members

Dr. John Smith

Professor of Computer Science

Specialization: Artificial Intelligence

Ph.D. in Computer Science, Stanford University

Dr. Smith's research focuses on machine learning algorithms, natural language processing, and AI ethics. He has published over 50 peer-reviewed papers and received multiple research grants from the National Science Foundation. Before joining Nexatech, he worked as a research scientist at Google AI.

Dr. Sarah Johnson

Professor of Business Administration

Specialization: Marketing

Ph.D. in Marketing, Harvard Business School

Dr. Johnson's research examines consumer behavior, digital marketing strategies, and brand management. She has consulted for major corporations including Coca-Cola, Procter & Gamble, and Amazon. Dr. Johnson is also the author of two textbooks on marketing management.

Dr. Michael Chen

Professor of Electrical Engineering

Specialization: Renewable Energy

Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering, Massachusetts Institute of Technology

Dr. Chen's research focuses on solar energy systems, energy storage technologies, and smart grid applications. He holds 15 patents in renewable energy technologies and has led several large-scale research projects funded by the Department of Energy. Dr. Chen is also a Fellow of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE).

Dr. Emily Rodriguez

Professor of Public Health

Specialization: Epidemiology

Ph.D. in Epidemiology, Johns Hopkins University

Dr. Rodriguez's research focuses on infectious disease epidemiology, global health, and health disparities. She has worked on public health initiatives in over 20 countries and has published extensively on HIV/AIDS prevention and treatment strategies. Dr. Rodriguez is a recipient of the Presidential Early Career Award for Scientists and Engineers.

Dr. David Kim

Professor of Economics

Specialization: International Economics

Ph.D. in Economics, University of Chicago

Dr. Kim's research examines international trade policies, global financial markets, and economic development. He has served as a consultant to the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank. Dr. Kim is the author of "Global Economic Integration: Challenges and Opportunities" and has received numerous awards for his research contributions.

Dr. Lisa Wang

Professor of Psychology

Specialization: Clinical Psychology

Ph.D. in Clinical Psychology, University of California, Berkeley

Dr. Wang's research focuses on anxiety disorders, trauma, and evidence-based therapeutic interventions. She maintains a part-time clinical practice and has developed innovative treatment protocols for post-traumatic stress disorder. Dr. Wang is a Fellow of the American Psychological Association and has received funding for her research from the National Institute of Mental Health.
