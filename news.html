News & Events

Stay updated with the latest news and upcoming events at Nexatech University

Latest News

Nexatech University Receives $2.5 Million Research Grant for Renewable Energy Projects

April 10, 2026

Nexatech University has been awarded a $2.5 million research grant from the Ministry of Science and Education for innovative work in renewable energy technologies. The funding will support cutting-edge research projects led by Dr. Michael Chen, Professor of Electrical Engineering, and his team of graduate students. The research will focus on developing more efficient solar energy systems and advanced energy storage solutions to address Kyrgyzstan's energy challenges.

New Graduate Programs in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence Announced

April 5, 2026

Nexatech University is excited to announce new graduate programs in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence, starting in the Fall 2026 semester. These programs are designed to meet the growing demand for skilled professionals in these high-demand fields. The curriculum includes courses in machine learning, data visualization, natural language processing, and ethical AI, taught by faculty with extensive industry experience.

Dr. Sarah Johnson Receives National Excellence in Teaching Award

March 28, 2026

Dr. Sarah Johnson, Professor of Business Administration, has been recognized with the National Excellence in Teaching Award for her innovative approach to business education and mentorship of students. Dr. Johnson was honored for her development of a project-based learning curriculum that connects students with local businesses to solve real-world challenges. She has also been instrumental in establishing the university's entrepreneurship center, which has supported the launch of several student-led startups.

University Hosts International Conference on Sustainable Development

March 15, 2026

Nexatech University recently hosted a three-day international conference on sustainable development, featuring leading experts from around the world discussing environmental challenges and solutions. The conference attracted over 300 participants from 25 countries, including researchers, policymakers, and industry leaders. Key topics included renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, and climate change adaptation strategies.

Student Team Wins National Robotics Competition

March 5, 2026

A team of engineering students from Nexatech University won first place in the national robotics competition, defeating 24 other teams from universities across Kyrgyzstan. The team's robot, designed to assist with search and rescue operations in disaster zones, impressed judges with its autonomous navigation capabilities and ability to operate in challenging environments. The victory earned the team a spot in the international robotics competition to be held in Tokyo later this year.

Campus Expansion Project Begins with New Research Center

February 20, 2026

Construction has begun on a new state-of-the-art research center and student residence hall as part of the university's ongoing campus expansion project. The 40,000-square-foot research center will house laboratories for engineering, computer science, and life sciences research, while the residence hall will provide accommodation for 300 students. The project, funded through a combination of government grants and private donations, is expected to be completed by the end of 2027.
